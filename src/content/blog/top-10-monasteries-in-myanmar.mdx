Myanmar has one of the densest concentrations of working Buddhist monasteries
(*kyaung*) in the world. They are living institutions — teaching, ordaining, and
anchoring communities — not museums. These ten stand out for architecture,
atmosphere, and importance.

## 1. Shwe Yan Pyay, Inle Lake

A teak monastery famous for its oval windows and golden light, where novice
monks study amid carved wood.

## 2. Bagaya Kyaung, Inwa (Ava)

A 19th-century teak monastery on hundreds of posts, blackened with age — one of
the finest surviving wooden monasteries in the country.

## 3. Shwenandaw Kyaung, Mandalay

The "Golden Palace Monastery," a richly carved teak hall that once formed part
of the royal palace.

## 4. Mahagandayon, Amarapura

One of the largest monastic colleges in Myanmar, where over a thousand monks
take the midday alms meal in procession.

## 5. Kyauk Taw Gyi area monasteries, Mandalay

Hill-foot monastic complexes tied to Mandalay Hill's long teaching tradition.

## 6. Taung Kalat (Mount Popa), near Bagan

A monastery perched atop a volcanic plug, reached by a long covered stair —
one of the most dramatic monastic sites in Asia.

## 7. Nga Phe Kyaung, Inle Lake

A historic wooden monastery on the lake, long known for its calm and its
collection of Buddha images.

## 8. Pa-Auk Forest Monastery, near Mawlamyine

A major international meditation monastery in the forest tradition, drawing
practitioners worldwide.

## 9. Kha Khat Wain Kyaung, Bago

One of Myanmar's largest monastic teaching institutions, central to monastic
education.

## 10. Sagaing Hill monasteries

A whole hillside of monasteries and nunneries — the spiritual retreat heartland
of the country, dense with quiet practice.

## Visiting working monasteries respectfully

These are active religious communities. Remove shoes and socks before entering
buildings and platforms, dress modestly (shoulders and knees covered), keep
voices low, and never pose with or touch monks or sacred images
disrespectfully. Photograph people only with consent; some halls forbid photos
entirely. Women should not touch monks or hand items directly to them.

## Timing

The cool, dry season (November–February) is most comfortable. Early morning is
the time for the alms round and the most atmospheric light; the midday meal
procession at Mahagandayon is a daily fixture but should be watched quietly
from the side, not crowded.

## A note on travel context

Conditions and access in Myanmar can change. Check current advice, travel with
local guidance, and prioritise visiting in a way that supports communities
rather than disrupts practice.
